Research

This project is from IAT 334, Interface Design, offered at Simon Fraser University. Using user research methods from IDEO Method Cards, I came up with a set of interview questions for my group partner and I to use with participants. Methods such as Survey & Questionnaires, Competitive Product Survey were used. An issue we came across was the lack of access to users of Trello. Due to time constraints, I suggested that interviews be done with individuals who have used any form of project management applications, such as Strike, Asana, as well as native operative system Calendar, Reminders, Tasks, Notes, and/or Clock applications. With this, we successfully collected sufficient data to analyze.

Execution

We noticed that our participants used various messenger platforms for team discussions like Facebook Messenger and Slack and concluded that this be our design focus: develop an instant messaging (IM) feature within Trello to avoid multiple application use. However, my group partner felt that any application can implement an IM feature and that ours should be unique. From this, I further developed the feature to include an option for Trello users to save brainstorming messages to a separate part of Trello that the group can assess and evaluate without unnecessary group chatter and being required to sift through messages. Fortunately, our feature impressed the teaching staff, resulting in an A-.

Reflection

From this project, I was a consistent problem solver through critical thinking. I had the opportunity to practice and develop a healthy working relationship with my project partner.
